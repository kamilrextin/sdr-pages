Pricing three motions separately at the board level

Postgres self-serve, Vitess enterprise, and Metal attach do not share a funnel, a payback curve, or a margin profile. A blended CAC view under-prices Vitess, over-prices Postgres, and hides Metal entirely. This document is a math skeleton, not a recommendation.

The three motions

1 · Postgres self-serve
Cost per net-new signup
$5 starter → $50 Metal → expansion
2 · Vitess enterprise
Cost per Stage-3 opportunity
Multi-stakeholder, AE-led, ABM-driven
3 · Metal attach
Cost per Metal conversion
Expansion motion on installed Postgres base

Each line item is measured two ways: blended (all S&M dollars across all motions, divided by that motion's output) and segmented (only that motion's directly-attributed S&M spend, divided by that motion's output). The delta between the two is where the pricing error lives.

Line item 1 · Postgres self-serve

Cost per net-new Postgres signup
Self-serve · PLG

Low-friction $5 starter ladder to $50 Metal and expansion. Most of the motion runs through content, docs, and paid — not AEs. Margin inside the funnel is thin until activation, so payback is almost entirely an acquisition-channel question, not a close-rate question.

Paid / content / MKT1 / podcast → signup → activation (first query) → expansion event ($5 → $50 → Metal)
Blended payback
(Total S&Mall motions) / (net-new signups)
÷ (ARPUsignup × GMself-serve)
= ~8.4 mo  illustrative
Segmented payback (per channel)
CACchannel = spendchannel / signupschannel
Paybackchannel = CAC / (ARPU × GM)
Organic: 2.1 mo · Paid: 14.6 mo
MKT1: 5.8 mo · Podcast: 3.4 mo

Line item 2 · Vitess enterprise

Cost per Vitess opportunity at Stage 3 (commit)
Enterprise · Sales-led

Multi-stakeholder, long cycle, high-ACV. CAC at Stage 3 (not signup, not SQL) is the earliest point where the opportunity is real enough to price. Blended lumps demand-gen, ABM, AE outbound, and partner-assist into one number — which hides the channel with the best payback and the one burning cash.

Demand-side content + ABM + AE outbound + partner intros → SAL → SQL → Stage 3 commit
Blended payback
(All S&M − self-serve-attributed spend) / Stage-3 opps
÷ (Avg won ACV × win rate × GMent)
= ~16.0 mo  illustrative
Segmented payback (per source)
Split by Stage-3 source: mktg / outbound / partner
CACsrc = spendsrc / Stage-3src
Mktg-sourced: 9 mo · Outbound: 22 mo
Partner-sourced: 6 mo

Line item 3 · Metal attach

Cost per Metal attach on installed Postgres base
Attach · Expansion

Today the cost to convert an existing $5/$50 Postgres customer into Metal is buried inside Postgres signup math. That is wrong on two counts: the Metal decision is an expansion trigger (not an acquisition event) and the cost driver is product + CS, not paid acquisition. Pricing it separately creates a real unit on the board.

Installed Postgres customer → product-usage trigger / PQL → CS or PLG nudge → Metal conversion

Where the ratios are soft — mark these up

  1. ARPUsignup denominator. Blending $5 starter and $50 Metal into one ARPU at signup depresses payback artificially. Suggest modeling signup ARPU at the 90-day mark (post-activation), not day 0.
  2. Content / brand allocation. MKT1 newsletter and podcast-referral are demand-gen that touches all three motions. Currently they will get fully loaded into Postgres signup CAC. Recommend splitting on touch-weighted basis, not first-touch.
  3. Vitess GM at payback time. Using won-ACV GM assumes linear ramp. Real payback on Vitess should be discounted for ramp (month 1 ACV < month 12 ACV). Flag for Eric: is Stage-3 the right commit point, or do we run payback off closed-won and accept longer feedback loops?
  4. Metal attach as acquisition vs. expansion. If we accept Metal is expansion, its CAC is a CS/PM cost, not a marketing cost. That changes who owns the payback number (Eric vs. product).
  5. Partner channel sample size. Partner-sourced Stage-3 count is low enough that the 6-mo payback number is noise. Two to three more quarters before this row is reliable.

Open questions for Eric

  • Does the current attribution model tag opportunities by source at Stage 3, or only at closed-won?
  • Are CS hours against installed Postgres customers tracked in a way that can be allocated to Metal conversions?
  • What is the current win-rate spread between mktg-sourced and outbound-sourced Vitess? If it's meaningful, the payback split above is the wrong shape.
  • Is there a way to measure podcast / MKT1 influence on Vitess Stage-3, or is it locked to self-serve signups in the current model?
  • What is the right denominator for Metal attach payback — incremental ARPU only, or full Metal ACV?
