THE PATTERN WE WATCH FOR

Greenfield DG into a fresh CRO mandate has a predictable failure mode.

We've watched this play out across PE-backed marketing teams in the first six months. It's not a hiring problem. It's a sequencing problem.

Month 0–2

The new hire takes inventory

Auditing HubSpot, mapping the ICP, talking to sales, picking tools, debating ABM platform vs. native HubSpot. Useful work. None of it ships pipeline.

Month 3–4

First plays go live, slowly

First content piece, first ABM list, first paid test. Numbers are noisy. The CRO asks for a forecast. Marketing has data on opens and clicks, not pipeline coverage.

Month 5–6

The credibility window closes

Two quarters in. CRO has revenue numbers, marketing has activity numbers. The DG manager is now defending the function instead of running it. Hiring loop reopens.

The fix isn't hiring faster. It's having a moving program for the new manager to walk into. Target accounts already named. Plays already wired. Measurement already in HubSpot. Their first 30 days go to running the engine, not building it.

MONTH 4 / THE CONVERSATION COMING UP

The pipeline-math conversation Terence is going to want.

Most marketing leaders inside Vista portcos end up running this exact review somewhere between month 4 and month 5. The leaders who walk in with the model already on a page get a different meeting than the ones who walk in with campaign metrics.

Pipeline coverage

3x or 4x of forward quota

By segment, by quarter, by sales rep. The number Terence cares about is whether the next two quarters of quota are buildable from the current pipeline, not last quarter's MQL volume.

Payback period

Months to recover CAC by channel

Vista's investment thesis assumes a payback target. Marketing leaders who can show payback by source per channel, not blended CAC, get budget arguments they can actually win.

ARR per marketing FTE

The headcount conversation

"Punches above its weight" is the answer to a question that's coming. The number that defends the structure is ARR-influenced or sourced per marketing FTE, trended over four quarters.
